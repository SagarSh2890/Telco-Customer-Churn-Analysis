Executive Summary – Telco Customer Churn Analysis
Problem Statement

The telecom company is facing a significant customer retention issue, with a considerable portion of users discontinuing services. High churn directly impacts revenue, customer lifetime value, and overall business growth, making it critical to identify key drivers behind customer attrition.

Objective of Analysis
To identify the key factors influencing customer churn
To analyze customer behavior across tenure, contract type, services, and payment methods
To provide data-driven insights that can help reduce churn and improve retention strategies
Overall Churn Rate

The dataset shows that 26.5% of customers have churned, while 73.5% are retained, meaning 1 out of every 4 customers leaves the service.

Customer Tenure Impact

Customers with tenure less than 12 months show the highest churn, with rates above 40%, whereas customers staying beyond 24 months have churn rates below 15%. This indicates that early-stage customers are at highest risk.

Contract Type Influence
Month-to-month contracts → ~42–45% churn
1-year contracts → ~10–12% churn
2-year contracts → ~3–5% churn
Long-term contracts significantly improve customer retention.
Internet Service Insights

Customers using fiber optic services have churn rates around 40%, compared to ~20% for DSL users, suggesting possible concerns related to pricing or service quality.

Support Services Effect
Without services (Tech Support, Online Security) → 35–45% churn
With services → 15–20% churn
Providing additional services clearly reduces churn risk.
Payment Method Trends

Customers using electronic check have the highest churn (~45%), while credit card and bank transfer users show lower churn (~15–20%).

Key Recommendations
Improve onboarding experience for new customers (0–12 months)
Promote long-term contracts to reduce churn by up to 40%
Bundle value-added services like tech support and security
Encourage customers to switch from electronic checks to more reliable payment methods
Conclusion

Customer churn is primarily driven by short tenure, flexible contracts, lack of support services, and payment behavior. By addressing these areas, the company can potentially reduce churn by 15–20% and improve overall customer retention and profitability.
